The open-source label player, now a native app — its own window, self-updating, and light on your machine.

Download for Mac
Universal (Apple Silicon + Intel) · ~9 MB · macOS 10.15+

Install — about a minute

  1. Open the downloaded .dmg and drag Aethra Kairos into your Applications folder.
  2. The first time only, right-click (or Control-click) the app and choose Open, then Open again in the dialog.
  3. That's it. It opens normally from now on, and quietly keeps itself up to date.
Why that first-launch warning? macOS shows it for any app not signed through Apple's paid Developer program. Aethra Kairos is open source — every line is public, so you (or anyone) can read exactly what it does. The warning is a signing formality, not a sign of anything unsafe. Signing/notarization is on the roadmap; until then, the one-time right-click → Open is all it takes.

What it is — and isn't

◇ What it is

  • A music player for the Aethra Kairos / ERRERlabs catalog
  • A live visualizer that moves to the music
  • Free and open source — forever

✓ What it does

  • Streams the catalog and draws the visuals
  • Keeps your hearts & play history on your device
  • Updates itself in the background

✕ What it never does

  • No account, no login — ever
  • No analytics, no tracking — nothing about your listening leaves your Mac
  • Doesn't read your files, and only uses the mic if you turn on the mic-reactive visual

The honest version

Your favorites and listening history are stored locally — in the app's own storage on your Mac — and are never uploaded. There are no third-party scripts, no telemetry, and no servers of ours between you and the music beyond streaming the audio itself. Don't take our word for it: the whole thing is open on GitHub.
